# Community Manager — How to Use the Ops Control Room Daily

This is your one-page guide. The app is the source of truth for Cohort 2 — if it isn't in the app, it didn't happen.

## First time in
1. Open the login link you were sent and sign in with the temporary password.
2. You'll be asked to set a new password (complete-setup). Do it once; you won't be asked again.
3. Add the app to your phone home screen so reminders reach you:
   - **iPhone (Safari):** Share → Add to Home Screen.
   - **Android (Chrome):** menu → Add to Home screen / Install app.
   - When prompted, **allow notifications** — this is how you get task reminders.

## What you can do
- **See everything** — every page is open to you (Dashboard, Participants, Reviews, Sessions, Ops, Community, etc.).
- **Edit two areas** — **Participants** and **Community** (CM Tracker). Other pages are read-only for you.
- **Work your tasks** — the **My Tasks** page is yours to action. Tasks tagged to your role (e.g. "CM Owner") show up there.

## Your daily 5 minutes
1. Open **My Tasks**. Anything **overdue** (amber) — do it or comment why it's blocked.
2. Open **Dashboard** → **Workload by owner**. If your role shows overdue, clear it.
3. Glance at **Dashboard** → **Today's attention** for red-risk students or review backlog.

## Your weekly rhythm (these are the tasks the system will nudge you on)
- **Midweek (Wed):** check in with the cohort, update each participant's attendance + submission on the **Participants** page, and flag who's gone silent or is stuck.
- **Deadline day (Thu):** confirm submissions are logged on **Participants** and on **Reviews**.
- **End of week (Fri):** fill in your **Community** (CM Tracker) row for the week — prompts posted, attendance/submissions updated, silent + stuck counts, escalations, energy level, key concerns, next actions, then mark **Status = Done** and **Weekly report sent = yes**.

## Flagging a student at risk (do this early, not at the end)
On **Participants**, open the student and set **Risk = Red** (or Amber). Setting Red automatically creates an outreach task on the owning CM — so flagging is also how you trigger help. Add a **Next action** so whoever picks it up knows the plan.

## Raising an escalation
Anything you can't resolve: bump the **Escalations raised** number on your weekly **Community** row and write it in **Key concerns**. Escalations surface on the Dashboard for the core team — that's the right way to get Olamide/leads involved, instead of DMing.

## Rules of the road
- Update the app the **same day** — attendance, submissions, contact. Stale data hides risk.
- Use **task comments** to say what's blocked; don't let an overdue task sit silently.
- One source of truth: the app, not the spreadsheet. Don't keep a side sheet.

Questions about access or a missing page? That's an admin thing — raise it with Olamide.
